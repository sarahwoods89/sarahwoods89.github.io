Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 April 2026

This privacy policy explains how Sarah Woods Art collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you visit this website or make a purchase. We are committed to handling your data transparently and in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable Irish and EU data protection law.

1. Who We Are

Sarah Woods Art is operated by Sarah Woods, an Irish artist based in Ireland. We are the data controller for personal data collected through this website.

Contact: sarahwoodsartist@gmail.com

2. What Data We Collect and Why

2.1 Newsletter Subscription

When you sign up for our newsletter, we collect your name and email address. We use this information to send you updates about new work, exhibitions, and collections.

  • Legal basis: Your consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR)
  • You can withdraw your consent and unsubscribe at any time by contacting us at sarahwoodsartist@gmail.com

2.2 Purchasing Artwork

When you purchase artwork, you are redirected to Stripe's secure checkout page. Stripe collects and processes your payment details directly; we do not receive or store your card information. We may retain your order details (such as your name, email address, and purchase amount) for accounting and legal purposes.

  • Legal basis: Performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR) and legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR)

2.3 Email Contact

If you contact us directly by email, we receive and store that correspondence in order to respond to your enquiry.

  • Legal basis: Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): responding to your enquiry

3. Third-Party Services

3.1 Stripe: Payment Processing

All purchases are processed by Stripe, Inc. Stripe is an independent data controller and their privacy policy governs how they handle your payment data. You can read it at stripe.com/privacy.

3.2 Google / YouTube

Our homepage includes embedded YouTube videos and uses the YouTube Data API to display recent content. When your browser loads these, Google may collect data and set cookies in accordance with their own privacy policy. You can read more at policies.google.com/privacy. If you have concerns about YouTube cookies, you can block third-party cookies in your browser settings.

3.3 Content Delivery Networks (CDNs)

This website loads certain libraries (including Bootstrap and jQuery) from third-party CDN servers. When your browser fetches these resources, the CDN provider may log your IP address as part of standard server logging.

3.4 GitHub Pages: Website Hosting

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect limited technical data (including IP addresses) in server access logs. See GitHub's Privacy Statement for details.

3.5 Instagram and Linktree

Our website links to our Instagram profile and Linktree page. If you follow those links, the privacy policies of those platforms apply. We do not share your data with Instagram or Linktree.

4. Cookies and Local Storage

This website does not set its own cookies.

We use your browser's local storage to remember your display preferences (such as dark mode). This data remains on your device only and is never transmitted to us or any third party.

Embedded YouTube videos may cause YouTube to set third-party cookies in your browser, even if you do not play a video. You can manage or block cookies at any time through your browser settings.

We do not use any advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics tools (such as Google Analytics).

5. Data Retention

  • Newsletter subscribers: your name and email are retained until you unsubscribe
  • Purchase records: retained for 7 years to meet accounting and legal obligations
  • Email correspondence: retained for as long as relevant to your enquiry

6. Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the EU, EEA, or UK, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification: ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure: ask us to delete your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restriction: ask us to limit how we process your data in certain circumstances
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Object: object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, please email sarahwoodsartist@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Ireland, this is the Data Protection Commission (dataprotection.ie).

7. International Transfers

Some third-party services we use (including Stripe and Google) may process your data outside the European Economic Area. Where this occurs, it is subject to appropriate safeguards under GDPR, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions by the European Commission.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data. All payment processing is handled by Stripe, which uses industry-standard security including TLS encryption. We do not store payment card data.

9. Children's Privacy

This website is not directed at children under 16 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision. Continued use of the website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
